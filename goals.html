Cepheus Mission Goals

Cepheus Mission Goals

Goal 1

Specific: To provide a highly innovative solution to the question “We need to leave Earth to survive. Where do we go? How do we go? How do we ensure survival upon arrival?”.

Measurable: We measure this by evaluating its novelty, feasibility, and effectiveness in addressing the core questions. This can be done by feedback from group members and teachers.

Achievable: To ensure that the proposed solution is achievable, we'll break down the project into manageable tasks, allocate time effectively, and use resource materials, and technology tools.

Relevant: The goal is relevant to the project's topic and the main question and aims to provide a practical and innovative solution.

Time-bound: This should be done before the due date of the final submission.

Goal 2

Specific: Design and develop an advanced spaceship prototype capable of safely transporting a crew of astronauts from Earth to Mars and ordinary people later on.

Measurable: Start the prototype by Week 7 and learn to use Blender to start making the prototype.

Achievable: Conduct thorough research on spacecraft design principles, materials, and technologies needed to ensure functionality and feasibility.

Relevant: The goal aligns with the assignment's theme of 'Beyond Earth, showing the critical aspect of transportation in space exploration.

Time-bound: Complete the basic outer design of the spaceship prototype design by Week 9 and complete the whole spaceship prototype design and presentation materials, including the visualizations, engineering specifications, and simulations by Week 15 leaving enough time for feedback from the teacher and revisions before final submission on Week 17.
