The journey can be divided into 3 parts:

1. Escaping Earth's Orbit

2. The travel from Earth to Mars

Escaping Earth's Orbit

In order to escape earth's orbit:

1. We are using Nuclear Thermal Propulsion

This is due to the fact that they are extremely propellant efficient and provide a high thrust(NASA 2024). This also consumes less fuel which allows us to reduce the weight of the rocket(Office of Nuclear Energy 2021).

2. How long will it take to build it ?

Since we have no constraints in terms of money and manpower it is theoretically possible for the rockets to be built in 5 years(National Air and Space Museum 2013).

3. How many will be built?

3 rockets will be built.

Travelling from Earth to Mars

In order to travel such a distance efficiently we are using Hohman's Orbit:

Reason:

1. Saves a lot of fuel

2. Has been done before

The Hohman's Orbit starts from the:

Low Earth Orbit- Travelling with the earth around the Sun.

Trans Mars Interjection- Fires engines to eject itself from low Earth orbit.

Interplanetary Transfer- End in an elliptical transfer orbit. Trajectory corrections are to fine tune it's path.

Mars encounter-Travels for 250 days until Mars orbit catches.

Mars Orbital Insertion-Fires engine to match velocity of Mars so that it can caught by its gravity (Jessy Sia). The entire journey will take 519 days(NASA).
